1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how TradeWizard ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website and use our trading platform and services (collectively, the "Services").

By using our Services, you consent to the use of cookies and similar technologies in accordance with this Cookie Policy. If you do not accept the use of cookies, please disable them as explained in this policy so they cannot be stored on your device when you use our Services.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognize your device and remember certain information about your visit, such as your preferences and settings.

Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site. They help us understand how our Services are being used and allow us to improve your experience.

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to report usage statistics, deliver advertisements, and so on. These cookies may include:

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences as follows:

6.1 Cookie Preference Center

You can manage your cookie preferences through our Cookie Preference Center where you can choose which categories of cookies you accept or reject. Essential cookies cannot be rejected as they are necessary for the Services to function properly.

6.2 Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow you to manage your cookie preferences through the browser settings. You can usually find these settings in the "options" or "preferences" menu of your browser. The following links provide information on how to modify cookie settings in the most popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Opera

6.3 Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" ("DNT") signal through which a user can indicate a preference not to have their web activities tracked. While we do our best to respect user preferences, we currently do not respond to DNT signals as there is no common industry standard for compliance.

6.4 Third-Party Opt-Out Tools

For cookies placed by third-party providers, you can also manage your preferences through these services:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Network Advertising Initiative Opt-out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance Opt-out
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance Opt-out (for EU users)

6.5 Consequences of Disabling Cookies

Please note that if you choose to disable cookies, you may not be able to access certain parts of our Services, and some features may not function properly. In particular, you will not be able to use features that require you to be logged in.

7. Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar technologies to track your use of our Services:

7.1 Web Beacons

Web beacons (also known as "clear gifs," "pixel tags," or "web bugs") are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies. While cookies are stored on your hard drive, web beacons are embedded invisibly on web pages. We may use web beacons to track user activities, determine the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns, and compile aggregate statistics about website usage.

7.2 Local Storage

We may use Local Storage (including HTML5 Local Storage and local shared objects) to store content information and preferences. Similar to cookies, Local Storage technologies can store data on your device for use by our Services. Various browsers may offer their own management tools for removing HTML5 Local Storage.

7.3 Fingerprinting

To avoid cookies in some cases, websites use other techniques that enable them to recognize devices on their return, often called fingerprinting. This involves collecting information about your device, browser, and plugins to create a unique "fingerprint." We may use device fingerprinting for security and fraud prevention purposes.

We encourage you to review this Cookie Policy periodically to stay informed about our use of cookies.
